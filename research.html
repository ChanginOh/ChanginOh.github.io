Research Interests

My research combines mathematical modelling and numerical simulation to investigate cancer dynamics and other biological systems. I am also interested in global optimization, Riemannian optimization, reinforcement learning, and universal physics-informed neural networks, with a particular focus on biological applications.

  • Mathematical Oncology
  • Tumour Dynamics

  • Optimization
  • Machine Learning

Current Directions

Age-dependent tumour-immune modelling

I develop mechanistic models for tumour growth and immune response, with emphasis on how aging may alter immune recruitment, cytotoxic activity, and tumour clearance. The goal is to connect biological hypotheses with parameterized dynamical systems and virtual population simulations.

Optimization for parameter estimation

Biological models often contain nonlinear, weakly identifiable, and constrained parameters. I am interested in robust global optimization strategies for fitting models to sparse biological datasets.

Geometric learning for molecular simulations

My work develops a Grassmann manifold framework to formulate linear collective-variable spaces for basis-invariant comparison of dimensionality reduction methods. The goal is to identify interpretable molecular coordinates that capture metastable states and conformational transitions.
